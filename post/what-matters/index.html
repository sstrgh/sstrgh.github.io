What Matters To Them

 Posted on May 26, 2020  |  2 minutes  |  389 words

As a new leader of 11 brilliant software engineers, I struggled to keep my team motivated given the current situation and I wrote this to share what worked for me in motivating the team.

COVID-19 is forcing businesses to scale down their efforts which means that we lose the opportunity to work on projects that matter to us, with people that mattered to us.It happened to me too this year. I lost a fantastic job working with brilliant people solving exciting problems. This crisis showed me the fragility of my efforts. That we can spend a lifetime working on our plans and sometimes plans don’t go as planned.

I learnt that the best way to overcome this fragility of our effort and to build resilience is to work on problems that matter to you. Whether it is to serve a purpose, community or even yourself.I bounced back because I found peace knowing despite how long or short my run with my team was, I worked on something that mattered to me, with people that mattered to me.

Recently, I was extended the opportunity to join SensorFlow as their Director of Software Engineering. SensorFlow builds hardware & software to help hotels become more energy efficient. Struggling with motivating the team, I reflected back on back on my recovery and decided that the first conversation with the team should encourage them to dig deep and ask what about their work at SensorFlow mattered to them. It helped me to understand what drives my team to come into the office everyday.

I then made sure to follow up with the team on this. So I’d also recommend organising regular 1 on 1 conversations with each member of the team where you discuss their motivation towards their work. This has helped me understand my team better and motivate them to do their best work here. I hope that this helps you too.

As a leader, it is our responsibility to provide our teams with an environment that helps them to work on things that matter to them. To help them understand that they matter here. If you have any other idea that have worked for you in helping your teams feel like their work matters and that they matter, I’d love to hear from you.
